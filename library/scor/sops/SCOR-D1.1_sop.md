# SOP — Define Design Chain Strategy
**Process ID:** SCOR-D1.1
**Framework:** SCOR-D | **Domain:** Design Chain
**Generated:** 2026-06-06

## Purpose
Process of defining the overall design chain strategy and objectives

## Triggers
- Change in Business Objectives
- Change in Customer Requirements
- Change in Market Trends
- Scheduled review of Design Chain Strategy

## Inputs Required
- business objectives
- customer requirements
- market trends

## Process Steps
1. IF Design Chain Strategy is not aligned with Business Objectives THEN revise Design Chain Strategy
2. IF Customer Requirements are not met by Design Chain Strategy THEN revise Design Chain Strategy
3. IF Market Trends indicate a change in customer needs THEN revise Design Chain Strategy

## Expected Outputs
- design chain strategy
- design chain objectives

## Business Rules
- Design Chain Strategy must be aligned with Business Objectives
- Design Chain Strategy must meet Customer Requirements
- Design Chain Strategy must be based on current Market Trends
- Design Chain Strategy must comply with EU AI Act if AI-driven design is used

## Exception Handling
- IF Design Chain Strategy development is delayed THEN notify stakeholders and revise project timeline
- IF Design Chain Strategy is not effective THEN revise Design Chain Strategy and re-evaluate KPIs

## Success Criteria
- Design Chain Strategy is aligned with Business Objectives
- Design Chain Strategy meets Customer Requirements
- Time-to-Market is within target range
- Design Quality meets target range
- Cost of Design is within target range

## Compliance Requirements
- EU AI Act if AI-driven design
