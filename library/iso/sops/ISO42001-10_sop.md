# SOP — AI Improvement — Nonconformity and Continual Improvement
**Process ID:** ISO42001-10
**Framework:** ISO 42001:2023 | **Domain:** ISO 42001
**Generated:** 2026-06-12

## Purpose
Management of AI-specific nonconformities including model failures, bias incidents, safety events and continual improvement of the AI management system

## Triggers
- New NonconformityReport ingested
- BiasIncident or SafetyEvent logged
- AuditFinding received
- PerformanceGap detected via KPI monitoring

## Inputs Required
- AI nonconformance reports
- bias incidents
- safety events
- audit findings
- performance gaps

## Process Steps
1. IF severity == 'critical' OR type == 'safety' THEN trigger immediate escalation and 24h corrective action
2. IF recurrence_count > 2 in 90 days THEN force model retraining and external audit
3. IF corrective_action_cycle_time > KPI_threshold THEN log failure and create new AIMSImprovement

## Expected Outputs
- AI corrective actions
- model retraining triggers
- AIMS improvements
- lessons learned
- updated AI controls

## Business Rules
- Every NonconformityReport must produce at least one CorrectiveAction within defined SLA
- All BiasIncident and SafetyEvent records require root cause and LessonLearned entry before closure
- ModelRetrainingTrigger must be logged with version, dataset hash and performance delta
- UpdatedAIControl changes require approval and version increment in AIMS registry

## Exception Handling
- If incident data is incomplete, route to data steward for enrichment before processing
- If regulatory notification required (EU AI Act high-risk), pause normal flow and execute compliance sub-process first

## Success Criteria
- AI corrective action cycle time <= target
- model incident recurrence rate decreases quarter-over-quarter
- AIMSImprovement implemented and verified in next audit

## Compliance Requirements
- ISO 42001:2023 Clause 10
- EU AI Act post-market monitoring
- NIST AI RMF continual improvement
